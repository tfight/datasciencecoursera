Butter Scones

Try this recipe for perfectly baked English scones. Munch with clotted cream and jam and a side of tea.

In this recipe, we'll guide you through the steps to create classic butter scones that are tender, flaky, and simply delicious. Whether you're enjoying them for breakfast or afternoon tea, these scones are sure to delight your taste buds. Follow our simple instructions and bring a touch of British elegance to your baking repertoire.

  • 450g self-raising flour
  • 100g unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 50g caster sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 225ml milk
  • Optional: additional milk for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 220°C/fan 200°C/gas mark 7. Lightly grease a baking sheet or line it with parchment paper.
  2. Combining Dry Ingredients
    In a large bowl, sift the self-raising flour and add the cubed cold unsalted butter. Use your fingertips to rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Adding Sweetness
    Stir in the caster sugar, ensuring it is evenly distributed throughout the mixture.
  4. Mixing Wet Ingredients
    In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and milk together. Pour the mixture into the dry ingredients and stir until a soft dough forms.
  5. Kneading the Dough
    Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Gently knead it a few times until it comes together and becomes smooth.
  6. Shaping the Scones
    Roll out the dough to a thickness of about 2cm. Use a round cookie cutter to cut out scones from the dough. Place them onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a little space between each scone.
  7. Baking Time
    Optional: Brush the tops of the scones with a little milk for a golden finish. Bake them in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until they are risen and golden brown.
  8. Serving Warm
    Remove the scones from the oven and transfer them to a wire rack to cool slightly. Serve them warm with clotted cream and your favorite jam.
